# Add auditable cash-secured put premium compensation floors

The candidate-price analysis may calculate historical minimum-premium compensation floors for downside Put scenarios without importing an option chain. For each matched historical path, expiration loss per share is `max(strike - projected expiration price, 0)`. A contiguous-block bootstrap provides the 95% confidence interval for mean expiration loss, and CVaR95 is the mean loss among the worst 5% of paths.

Four per-share historical references are reported: the upper 95% mean-loss bound plus transaction cost; that statistical reference plus a cash-secured capital-return hurdle; and two tail-stress references that add 10% or 25% of `CVaR95 - mean expiration loss`. Defaults are a $0.03 per-share execution cost and a 10% annual capital-return hurdle using the full strike as cash collateral. The annual hurdle is user-adjustable and reprices the derived references without rerunning historical paths or bootstrap. The 10% and 25% tail loadings are explicit risk-preference assumptions, not statistically calibrated option values. These assumptions and each formula component remain visible and exported. They are secondary historical diagnostics, not theoretical option values, Safety Grades, or recommendations.

Premium comparison requires at least 20 effective positive-payoff observations, where effective events equal the observed payoff rate times the horizon's effective sample size. Below that evidence gate, the interface and exports return `insufficient-evidence` rather than a favorable comparison, while retaining the raw references for audit. When no historical path produces a positive expiration payoff, ordinary bootstrap and historical CVaR remain zero because resampling cannot invent an unseen loss. An unseen-event stress reserve would require a separate explicit modeling decision.

An optional manually entered executable net bid or plausible limit fill can be located relative to the four references without rerunning historical paths. Reaching a reference is never described as evidence that the option is cheap or worth selling. Live option chains, implied volatility, Greeks, broker margin, Naked Call premium floors, quantities, and order entry remain excluded. Assignment-budget calculations continue to ignore premium conservatively.
